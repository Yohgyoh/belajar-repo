Apple Reportedly Cooking Up a Cheaper MacBook with an iPhone Chip!

Heads up, Apple fans and budget hunters! Whispers are getting louder that Apple is secretly working on a brand-new, more affordable MacBook line, possibly hitting shelves for under $999. This juicy rumor comes from analyst Ming-Chi Kuo, who suggests this budget-friendly MacBook, powered by an iPhone chip, could drop by late 2025 or early 2026.

Now, here's where it gets interesting: unlike most MacBooks that run on their own M-series chips (think M1, M2, M3), this rumored cheaper version might pack an A18 Pro chip. Yep, that's the *same* chip expected to power the iPhone 16 Pro!

This move looks like Apple's smart play to slash production costs and open up the MacBook market to a much wider audience, especially students and first-time laptop buyers. The word on the street is this budget MacBook will sport a 13-inch display and come in a few color options. It’s also expected to be even thinner and lighter than the current MacBook Air, though we're still in the dark about other tech specs like RAM, storage, or battery life.

Kuo even hinted that Apple is planning to churn out between 5 to 7 million units in 2026, which pretty much screams confidence in this affordable MacBook's appeal.

First Mac with an iPhone Chip?

If these rumors pan out, this MacBook would be the very first Mac to use an iPhone chip instead of the usual M-series. The A18 Pro itself is built on TSMC’s second-gen 3nm process, promising better power efficiency and souped-up AI performance.

Early benchmark leaks show the A18 Pro chip hitting a single-core score of around 3500 and a multi-core score of about 8780. That puts it right up there with the early M1 chip from 2020. What does that mean for you? Well, that kind of power is more than enough for everyday tasks like web Browse, document editing, video streaming, and even some light photo editing without any significant slowdowns. Most average users won't feel a thing!

Why This Makes Sense for Apple

Apple hasn’t officially commented on these rumors, but analysts are saying this move totally makes sense. It would let Apple fill a gap in the market between the iPad and the MacBook Air, and also go head-to-head with Chromebooks, which are super popular with students right now.

According to KompasTekno (via Toms Hardware, Sunday, July 6, 2025), this device is also expected to run the full macOS operating system, not iPadOS. That’s key – it means you’re still getting the full Apple computing experience.

If this rumor turns out to be true, launching a cheaper MacBook with an iPhone chip would be a seriously innovative move for Apple, shaking up their product line strategy big time. After all, Apple has never released a MacBook priced under $999 (the current cheapest MacBook Air M2 starts higher). With this new model, it looks like they’re finally ready to jump into the truly affordable laptop territory.
